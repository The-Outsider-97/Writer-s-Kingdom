style theories

Show, don't tell

Describe sensations, actions, and dialogue to convey emotion — let readers infer.

Stream of consciousness

Narrative that flows through a character's thoughts, often disjointed but deeply personal.

Epistolary

Told through documents: letters, diary entries, emails — creates intimacy.

Chekhov's gun

Every element must be necessary; remove anything irrelevant to the story.

story structures

Three‑act

Setup, confrontation, resolution — classic Hollywood rhythm.

The Hero's Journey

Departure, initiation, return — Campbell's monomyth with stages like 'crossing the threshold'.

Fichtean curve

Rapid rising action, few falling actions — many modern novels use this.

In medias res

Start in the middle of action, then fill in backstory via flashbacks.

Save the Cat

15 beat structure (opening image, catalyst, midpoint, etc.) for screenplays.
